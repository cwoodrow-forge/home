# Three bucket strategy for retirees

## Big Idea 
---
The three bucket strategy uses three 'conceptual' funding buckets to divide retirement assets into one of three categories
- Funding for day-to-day spend (1 - 2 years). Let's call that the [[Liquidity Bucket]] Normally cash and savings would sit in here.
- All-weather medium investments (3 - 7 years). Let's cll that the [[Lifestyle Bucket]]. This bucket is used to top up annually the Liquidity bucket. Draw from this if stock returns are down and additional top up of funds needed. Normally high-interest and ETFs or Dividend stocks would sit in here. Use these to ensure leveraging personal allowances on Dividends, Savings and Capital Gains tax. This bucket is the buffer to smooth out market troughs.
- Growth bucket (8+ years). Let's call that [[Longevity Bucket]]. Avoid drawing from this bucket unless returns have exceeded plan. If so top up funding from this instead of the all-weather bucket. Normally ISAs, Pensions and globally diversified stocks would sit in here. This is the investment engine of the entire portfolio.

This helps with two goals:
1. Managing psychological fears of drawing down funds based on an uncertain future. Allows me to feel spend is managed in line with a plan and adjustable for mid-term market shocks. The pots are designed to provide abstraction and protect the growth engine from decisions based on fear or reaction to the market. The Lifestyle bucket provides a flexible 'buffer' between liquidity and longevity funds to avoid 'panic' draw downs from the growth engine. Now everything is considered not reactive.
2. Places focus on tax-efficiency in the 'bridge' years between retiring at 60 and the state pension at 67. The bridge is designed to cover the annual spend shortfall between target spend and defined benefit pension in absence of a regular work income, whilst at same time protecting the growth engine assets.

Bridge = Defined Benefit + Liquidity/year x number of years until state pension
Example: If I want to have £30,000 a year income.
Bridge requirement (£98k) = (£30k - £16k) x 7

The bridge funding required can then be appropriately divided between the three buckets, likely weighted towards Liquidity (years 1 -2) and Lifestyle (3 - 7), although shouldn't ignore those 7 years of potential growth opportunities either.
