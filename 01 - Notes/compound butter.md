## Description

A compound butter is softened unsalted butter blended with flavourings — herbs, spices, aromatics, acids — then rolled in cling film and chilled until firm. Slice off rounds to finish grilled meat or fish, melt into sauces, or spread straight from the fridge. Makes almost any main feel restaurant-quality for almost zero effort.

## Base Technique

1. Leave butter at room temperature for 30 minutes until genuinely soft
2. Beat with a wooden spoon or fold with a spatula — do not melt
3. Add flavourings and mix thoroughly
4. Tip onto cling film, roll into a log roughly 3cm diameter, twist ends to seal
5. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or freeze — keeps 3 months)
6. To serve: slice 1cm rounds and lay on hot food just before plating

### Café de Paris Butter
Classic Swiss butter. Rich, complex, brilliant on anything off a grill.

**Pairs with:** [[Steak Sarnie]], [[Medallions of Beef with Morels, Marsala and creme fraiche sauce]]

| Flat-leaf parsley, chopped | 2 tbsp |
| Thyme leaves | 1 tsp |
| Marjoram | 1 tsp |
| Lemon zest | ½ lemon |
| Paprika | ½ tsp |
| Curry powder | ¼ tsp |
