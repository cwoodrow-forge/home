# Liquidity Bucket

## What is it?
The liquidity bucket is the principle day-to-day spending fund. It is normally structured to hold around two years of required cash funds as part of the bridging strategy to maximise tax allowances in the years between retirement at 60 and the state pension becoming available at 67. 
This is the period when no work income is coming in, but equally offers an opportunity to maximise tax efficiencies in other assets, given at 67 the state pension alone will take up most. That being said, the [[Defined Benefit Pension]] will already have used all of the personal income tax allowance before any other income is factored in. That's why it makes sense to use the bridging strategy to draw funds from cash and savings in the first years, avoiding tax on CGT and Dividends outside of allowances.

## 🥇 Liquidity (0–2 years)

- Cashflow engine
- No volatility
- You _expect_ to spend this

👉 “This money will be used”
### What funds make up Liquidity bucket
Principally:
- **Cash** (current accounts) - This is already taxed. So holding enough cash to support predicted cashflow means no additional basic tax is due beyond the difference between the defined benefit annual annuity and personal tax allowance. 
- **Savings** (Easy access or limited access) - Only the interest is taxable. So long as the annual interest is below personal allowance (£1000 for basic tax payer £500 for higher rate tax payer), no additional tax will be due. Savings provide easy access to liquidity whilst offsetting some of the effects of inflation on holding too much cash.
### 🔄 How it connects to the system

#### Monthly:

- Spend from **Liquidity bucket**
#### Annually:

- Refill Liquidity from:
    - [[Lifestyle Bucket]] (if markets down)
    - [[Longevity Bucket]] (if markets up)
