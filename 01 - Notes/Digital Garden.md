## What is a second brain?
A system to store information, aid in learning and more importantly, reduce cognitive overload in todays rapidly evolving world.
Second Brains, and as a result 'Digital Gardens' are spaces and methods for capturing notes, thoughts, ideas in a consistent way using tools, tags and techniques that creates a repository of highly searchable, maintained and ever growing personal knowledge.

# Knowledge Management Concepts

## Personal Knowledge Repository (PKR)
A **Personal Knowledge Repository (PKR)** is a system for storing, organizing, and retrieving personal knowledge in a structured way. It serves as an external memory, capturing information from various sources and making it accessible for future use.

### Core Principles:
- **Centralization** – All important knowledge is stored in one place.
- **Organization** – Information is categorized using tags, folders, or links.
- **Retrieval** – Data should be easily searchable and usable.
- **Curation** – The repository is actively maintained and refined over time.

### Features:
- Can be digital (e.g., Obsidian, Notion, Evernote) or physical (notebooks, binders).
- Includes personal notes, references, articles, and insights.
- Focuses on knowledge consumption and storage.

---
## Building a Second Brain (BASB)
**Building a Second Brain (BASB)** is a structured method for knowledge management developed by Tiago Forte. It focuses on organizing and synthesizing information to improve creativity, learning, and productivity.

### Core Principles:
- **CODE Framework**:
  - **C**apture – Save useful ideas and information.
  - **O**rganize – Structure notes for easy access.
  - **D**istill – Summarize and refine key insights.
  - **E**xpress – Use the knowledge in creative ways (writing, projects, etc.).
- Progressive summarization to highlight key insights.
- Focus on actionability—notes should support projects and goals.

### Features:
- Prioritizes useful, actionable knowledge over mere storage.
- Encourages regular review and synthesis of information.
- Often implemented using apps like Obsidian, Notion, or Evernote.

---

## Digital Garden
A **Digital Garden** is a public or private collection of evolving notes, ideas, and insights. Unlike traditional blogs, digital gardens are non-linear, interconnected, and constantly updated.

### Core Principles:
- **Evergreen Notes** – Notes are continuously refined and updated.
- **Interconnectivity** – Notes link together, forming a web of ideas.
- **Non-linearity** – No rigid hierarchy; instead, notes grow organically.
- **Public Learning** – Often shared online to foster discussion and collaboration.

### Features:
- Notes evolve over time rather than being static.
- Emphasizes learning in public (if shared).
- Implemented in tools like Obsidian, TiddlyWiki, or websites with bi-directional linking.

## Relationships Between These Concepts
| Concept  | Focus | Key Features | Relation to Others |
|----------|-------|-------------|--------------------|
| **PKR** | Storage & Retrieval | Organized, categorized knowledge | Forms the foundation for structured knowledge management. |
| **BASB** | Knowledge Utilization | Actionable, synthesized insights | Uses PKR as storage but adds layers of organization and expression. |
| **Digital Garden** | Knowledge Growth | Interconnected, evolving notes | Can be built on a PKR but focuses on public or semi-public exploration of ideas. |

### Summary:
- A **PKR** is the base layer—a place to collect and organize information.
- **BASB** builds upon a PKR, adding structure for creative work and productivity.
- A **Digital Garden** is an evolving knowledge network, often shared publicly, that encourages continuous learning.
