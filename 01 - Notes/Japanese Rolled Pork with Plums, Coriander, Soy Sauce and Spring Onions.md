## Description
This #asian dish could also be a #starter depending how many are made. It's technically an apetizer, clean and low on fat.
## Method
Wash the plums, cut, twist and remove stones. Dice. Heat oil in a pan and fry start anise, ginger and cloves for 1 minute. Add the plums and sugar with a couple tablespoons water. Cover and simmer low for 20 minutes until pulpy. Cool in fridge.
**Note:** Or just cheat and use a Hoisin sauce instead, although it's not the same.
Trim pork loin so super lean. Cut into slices and bash each between clingfilm until thin. Approx thickness of two beer-mats.
Lay out then season with plum sauce, coriander and roll each up like a mini swiss-roll. Put into a bamboo steamer and steam for 10 - 15 minutes. 
Serve on bed of finely sliced spring onions, doused with lots of soy sauce.
Could maybe flesh out with a timbale of rice. Maybe sushi rice and some avocado?

## Ingredients
- 10 plums
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 star anise
- 2 thumb-sized ginger, peeled and grated.
- 2 cloves
- 2 heaped tbsp muscovado sugar
- pork loin
- handful fresh coriander
- spring onions
- soy sauce
