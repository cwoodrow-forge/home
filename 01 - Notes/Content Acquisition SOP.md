# Content Acquisition SOP

**Goal:** Build a consistent habit of consuming high-quality content and capturing key insights into your vault.

### Workflow Summary
1. **Consume** — Read, watch, or listen with intention
2. **Highlight** — Capture the best insights as you go
3. **Drop into Inbox** — Paste highlights into a new note in `00 - Inbox/`
4. **Process** — Move to `01 - Notes/` as a Literature Note

---

### What to Consume

Prioritize depth over novelty. A useful framework:

1. **Books** — Deep, structured insights that compound over time
2. **Articles** — Focused, specific ideas
3. **Newsletters** — Curated, regular perspective
4. **Videos & Podcasts** — Good for frameworks and stories
5. **Tweets & short-form** — Low depth, use sparingly

Apply **Lindy's Law**: the longer an idea has existed, the more likely it is to stay relevant. Prioritize timeless over trending.

---

### How to Capture

As you consume, capture the highlights that matter most:

- Highlight sparingly — only what genuinely surprises, challenges, or confirms something important
- Add a brief note next to each highlight: *why does this matter? How could I use this?*
- Don't just collect — interpret as you go

**On the go:** Drop rough ideas and half-formed thoughts into `00 - Inbox/` immediately. A voice note transcript, a quick bullet, a copied passage — anything. The Inbox is your zero-friction capture point.

---

### How to Process

Once you're ready to process a capture:

1. Create a new note in `01 - Notes/` using the **Literature Note Template**
2. Add `categories: [[Literature Notes]]` and the right `type:` (book / article / video / podcast)
3. Paste your highlights into the **Highlights** section
4. Move on — you'll summarize later during your weekly note session

---

### Build the Habit

- **Daily:** 20–30 min of intentional reading or listening. Capture as you go.
- **Weekly:** Process 1–3 literature notes (highlights → summaries → permanent notes)
- **Start small:** One highlight per session is enough to begin. Consistency beats volume.
