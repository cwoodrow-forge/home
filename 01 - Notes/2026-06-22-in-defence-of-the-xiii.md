# In Defence of the XIII — Why Ultramarines in the Heresy

## Premise

Pick Ultramarines in a hobby conversation and someone will raise an eyebrow. They're the "safe" choice, the tutorial example, the default. In 40k that reputation is at least partly earned.

But this is the Heresy. And in the Heresy, the XIII Legion is almost the opposite of their 40k image.

This article makes the case — personal and lore-based — for why Ultramarines in the Age of Darkness is actually one of the more interesting choices you can make, and why I made it.

---

## Core Argument

The Heresy-era Ultramarines are defined by what they *miss*. They don't make it to Terra. Guilliman doesn't arrive in time. The Five Hundred Worlds hold, but hold alone, while every other loyal Legion races toward the Siege.

There's no glory in that. There's stubbornness, duty, and an almost Sisyphean act of holding the line for a battle you can't reach. The Legion that everyone associates with the rulebook ends up being the one written out of the war's climax entirely.

That's not blandness. That's tragedy.

And underneath all the lore: these are the Space Marines of my childhood. The blue armour on the original Space Marine box. The army in the first Epic Armageddon photograph I ever painted.
