## Summary

### Summary 1 — Wealth is assets, not income
Most people optimize for salary. Naval argues for building assets — things that earn money while you sleep (businesses, code, media, investments). The goal is to own equity in something that scales without your direct time.

### Summary 2 — Specific knowledge can't be outsourced
Generic skills get commoditized. Specific knowledge — built from genuine curiosity at the intersection of multiple domains — is what creates leverage. It can't be taught, only earned through lived experience.

### Summary 3 — Code and content are zero-cost leverage
Before the internet, leverage required people or capital. Now: one piece of content or one line of code can reach millions with no additional effort per person.

### Summary 4 — Happiness is a learnable skill
Naval treats happiness as a practice, not a feeling that happens to you. Default state is calm. Unhappiness comes from wanting things to be different than they are — and this reaction can be trained out.
