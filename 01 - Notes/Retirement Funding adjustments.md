# Retirement Funding adjustments

## Why do I need to balance my retirement portfolio?
In simple terms, because market volatility, inflation and tax can drastically erode it if not managed efficiently.  
For example: Having large number of LLOY shares plus a FTSE 100 ETF
👉 Combined, this means:

- Heavy **UK concentration**
- High **dividend tax exposure (outside ISA)**
- Lower diversification vs global markets

Selling down a position like Lloyds creates a moment of choice.

Left alone, it tends to drift—either sitting as cash, or being pushed back into another growth asset out of habit. But neither really improves the structure.

Used deliberately, though, it becomes a way of reinforcing the system. Strengthening the layer that protects your spending, rather than adding more weight to the part that’s already doing the heavy lifting.

It’s less about replacing one investment with another, and more about redistributing responsibility across the portfolio.
### 🔄 What to do with it in your plan

Keep (but rebalance)
- It can remain part of Longevity
👉 But:
- Target **<15–20% of total portfolio** in UK equities

### Example:
Say you crystallise:
👉 £50k of LLOY

Allocation flow:
#### 🟡 £30k → Lifestyle bucket

- £15k bonds
- £15k multi-asset
#### 🟢 £20k → Longevity (optional)

- Global equity ETF / fund

## As you crystallise LLOY:

### Step 1 — Estimate CGT immediately

Example:

- Gain realised → £10,000
- After allowance → taxable £7,000
- Tax → ~£1,300–£2,300 (depending on rate)
### Step 2 — Set it aside immediately

Move that amount into:

👉 **A separate “tax pot” inside Liquidity**. Savings account could be good for this. DON'T spend it.

- Easy access savings
- Clearly labelled mentally (or literally)
### Step 3 — Do NOT invest this

- Not bonds
- Not multi-asset
- Not Premium Bonds

👉 It is spoken for

Tax has a habit of being treated as something abstract—calculated later, dealt with when it arrives.
But in a system like this, it’s better thought of as already gone.
The moment you crystallise a gain, a portion of that money stops being yours in any meaningful sense. Treating it that way—setting it aside immediately—keeps the rest of the structure clean.

It removes ambiguity. And in a plan that depends on clear sequencing, that clarity matters.
