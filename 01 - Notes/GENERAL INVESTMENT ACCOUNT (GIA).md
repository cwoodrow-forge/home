## GENERAL INVESTMENT ACCOUNT (GIA)

Any extra funds that are left over after considering emergency fund, pension and ISAs could be considered for 'investing' in general stocks, index funds like [[ETF]] or Mutuals, Bonds or other investment products.
Any gains made on these will be subject to capital gains tax (CGT) which is currently applied to gains from shares including any dividends if they exceed personal allowance.
#### Personal allowance:
- **Dividends** £500
- **Capital gains** £3000
- **Cash** and **savings** PSA
	- **Basic** Rate tax payer: £1000
	- **Higher** Rate tax payer: £500

> The **PSA applies to interest** from:
> - Bank accounts (current, savings)
> - Fixed-rate bonds
> - Credit union accounts
> - Peer-to-peer lending interest    
> - Corporate bonds and gilts
