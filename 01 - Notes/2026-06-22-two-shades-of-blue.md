# Two Shades of Blue — Painting the XIII at Two Scales

## Premise

I paint Ultramarines twice. Once at 28mm for Age of Darkness — five-step armour, airbrushed modulation, hour-per-model pacing. Once at 6mm for Legions Imperialis — a single coat of Talassar Blue over Grey Seer primer, done in an evening.

Same legion. Same blue. Completely different relationship with a paintbrush.

This is about what that compression reveals — about the hierarchy of detail, about which decisions survive the scale change, and about what it means to call them "the same army."

---

## Core Argument

The interesting thing about painting the same faction at two radically different scales isn't the technical challenge. It's the *philosophical* one: you're forced to decide, explicitly and repeatedly, what actually matters about how your army looks.

At 28mm you can agonise over panel gradients. At 6mm you cannot. So what do you hold onto? The answer reveals something about what you're actually trying to achieve.

In my case: the cold blue tone (hence Grey Seer primer before anything else), the hierarchy of white for veterans, the gold studs. Everything else — the subtle wash variation, the bolter casing colour shift, the holster leatherwork — simply disappears. And the models still look like Ultramarines.

That's either humbling or liberating, depending on your mood.
