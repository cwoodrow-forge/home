## Description
Caprese elevated with burrata instead of mozzarella. The creamy interior of the burrata spills out when cut and mingles with the tomato juices and olive oil — that's the dish. Five minutes, zero cooking. Quality of ingredients is everything here.

## Ingredients *(serves 2)*
- 2 balls burrata (approx 250g total), at room temperature
- 4–5 large ripe tomatoes — heirloom or good vine if possible
- Large handful fresh basil leaves
- 3–4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil — use the best you have
- 1–2 tbsp balsamic glaze
- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper
- Crusty bread or rolls to serve

## Method
1. Remove burrata from the fridge 30 minutes before serving — the creamy centre must be soft.
2. Slice tomatoes into ¼-inch discs and arrange on a serving platter. Season with flaky salt and a drizzle of olive oil.
3. Pat burrata dry and place whole in the centre of the platter (or cut into thick pieces).
4. Tear basil by hand — never cut, a knife blackens it — and scatter over.
5. Drizzle generously with olive oil and balsamic glaze. A final crack of black pepper.
6. Serve immediately. Do not assemble in advance.

## Notes
- Heirloom tomatoes make a significant difference — worth sourcing
- The balsamic glaze (thick, reduced) is better here than raw balsamic vinegar
- Bread rolls are ideal for mopping up the oil and burrata cream
