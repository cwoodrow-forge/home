## Summary
*Reformulated in my own words — each concept ready to extract into a permanent note.*

### Summary 1 — The Zettelkasten is a thinking partner, not a storage system
Most people use note-taking apps as filing cabinets. The value of the Zettelkasten comes from the *connections* between notes, not from the notes themselves. A note that doesn't connect to anything is essentially dead.

### Summary 2 — Writing is the core activity, not the output
Ahrens inverts the typical view: writing isn't what you do *after* you understand something — it's how you come to understand it. The act of translating an idea into words forces clarity.

### Summary 3 — Permanent notes must be in your own words
Copying highlights = passive collection. Rewriting in your own words = active synthesis. If you can't paraphrase it, you didn't understand it.
