## WORKPLACE PENSION
Is a mix of non-contributory Defined Benefit and contribution based 'Pensions Extra', which is a SIPP.
### SIPPs

Self Invested Pension Plan (SIPP) is basically a private pension. You can have as many as you like, however in total the maximum you can pay into them in any tax year is £60,000 or 100% salary (whichever is lower).
The maximum amount you can pay in also includes any contributions to workplace pensions.

Example: I contribute £30K a year using salary sacrifice to 'Pensions Extra' workplace defined contribution scheme. I can pay a maximum £30K into a SIPP.

Important: This also includes MY contribution to any Defined Benefit scheme, so must work that out also and add that to the total.

### Drawdown
You can draw down funds from a SIPP from age 55. You can currently draw down 25% of the total pot as a tax-free lump sum and the remainder will be subject to tax at marginal tax rate (basically whatever tax bracket you end up in for that year based on income).

### Pension allowance
The annual allowance is the maximum amount of pension savings an individual can make each year without an annual allowance charge applying. This includes pension contributions made by the individual, their employer, a company or a third-party.

Individuals are subject to a tax charge on the amount of any contribution paid (personally, by their employer or a third-party) in excess of the annual allowance each year. The tax charge will be at the individual's marginal rate of income tax. This also applies to the value of any benefit increase under a defined benefit or cash balance scheme over the annual allowance. If an annual allowance charge is due this will usually be dealt with through the individual's self-assessment tax return.

Current annual allowance is £60,000

### Pension input amount
The pension input amount for a defined contribution plan is the amount of contributions made during the pension input period.

For defined benefit schemes, the pension input amount is the value of benefits accrued over the pension input period.
