An Exchange Traded Fund, or (ETF) is a type of 'Index Fund'. Although they may follow different rules, in essence both ETF and Mutual Funds are a portfolio of diversified stocks traded as a whole that track the index. If on average the companies included in the ETF do well, the overall fund does well. Returns may not be as good as the top performing companies in the fund, but the risks from volatility of a small number of companies is reduced.

## Things to be aware of
There are two types of Index Funds; Mutual and ETFs. 
- With a Mutual fund you 'buy' at the end of day price and often there's a minimum investment price. They also tend to have slightly higher fees. They are more invest and forget type index funds.

- With an ETF they constantly track the stock market and you but them in the form of 'shares' on a broker platform. Fees are generally lower and you (generally) only buy whole shares.

## Trading within the ISA wrapper
You can sell the shares held in a stocks and shares ISA without being liable for capital gains tax. It briefly gets converted to cash that remains within the isa wrapper. So you can reinvest that (or re-balance) the portfolio without losing the benefit of the tax wrapper.

### 1. Broad Market ETFs

These track a major stock index and provide broad exposure to the market.

- **SPDR S&P 500 ETF (SPY)**
    
    - Tracks the **S&P 500 Index**, covering the 500 largest U.S. companies.
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia, Alphabet, etc.
        
    - **Diversification:** High, across multiple sectors like technology, healthcare, and finance.
        
- **Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF (VTI)**
    
    - Covers the **entire U.S. stock market**, including large-, mid-, and small-cap stocks.
        
    - **More diversified** than SPY, with over 4,000 holdings.
        

---

### 2. International ETFs

These provide exposure to stocks outside the U.S.

- **iShares MSCI Emerging Markets ETF (EEM)**
    
    - Focuses on **emerging markets** (China, India, Brazil, etc.).
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Tencent, Alibaba, Samsung, Taiwan Semiconductor.
        
    - **Diversification:** High, but with country risk (China-heavy).
        
- **Vanguard FTSE Developed Markets ETF (VEA)**
    
    - Covers **developed economies** outside the U.S. (Europe, Japan, Canada).
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Nestlé, Toyota, Roche, HSBC.
        

---

### 3. Sector-Specific ETFs

These target specific industries.

- **Technology: Invesco QQQ (QQQ)**
    
    - Tracks the **Nasdaq-100 Index** (tech-heavy).
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Amazon, Tesla.
        
    - **Diversification:** Low (heavily focused on tech).
        
- **Healthcare: Health Care Select Sector SPDR (XLV)**
    
    - Focuses on healthcare stocks.
        
    - **Top Holdings:** UnitedHealth, Johnson & Johnson, Pfizer, Merck.
        
- **Energy: Energy Select Sector SPDR (XLE)**
    
    - Covers oil, gas, and energy companies.
        
    - **Top Holdings:** ExxonMobil, Chevron, ConocoPhillips.
        

---

### 4. Dividend ETFs

These focus on companies with strong dividend payouts.

- **Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIG)**
    
    - Invests in companies with a history of increasing dividends.
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Johnson & Johnson, Microsoft, Procter & Gamble.
        
- **Schwab U.S. Dividend Equity ETF (SCHD)**
    
    - Focuses on high-dividend U.S. stocks.
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Broadcom, PepsiCo, Coca-Cola, Verizon.
        

---

### 5. Bond ETFs

These provide exposure to fixed-income investments.

- **iShares U.S. Aggregate Bond ETF (AGG)**
    
    - Covers a mix of U.S. government and corporate bonds.
        
    - **Diversification:** Good for balancing stock market risk.
        
- **Vanguard Short-Term Bond ETF (BSV)**
    
    - Focuses on short-term bonds (lower risk than AGG).
        

---

### 6. Thematic & Alternative ETFs

These focus on trends or alternative assets.

- **ARK Innovation ETF (ARKK)**
    
    - Invests in disruptive technology (AI, biotech, etc.).
        
    - **Top Holdings:** Tesla, Roku, Coinbase, Zoom.
        
    - **Higher risk but high-growth potential.**
        
- **SPDR Gold Shares (GLD)**
    
    - Tracks the price of **gold**, used as an inflation hedge.
