## EMERGENCY FUND

An easily accessible cash fund that's available immediately as a fund for unexpected outgoings. Normally 3 months of **essential outgoings** is recommended if finances are stable.

Quick example:  
If your essential expenses are £2,000 per month, aim for £6,000–£12,000 saved in easily accessible cash (like a high-interest savings account).
Note - Cash does not necessarily mean current account, but an interest yielding fund. This could be savings account or a well-paying current account like Santander.

## Planned Expenditure

Any intended expenditure on the house, or for replacing high-cost items (such as laptops, phones, major kitchen equipment etc). Planned expenditure should include:
- Home renovation projects like Kitchen, Bathroom, Patio
- Maintenance projects, like pointing, loft insulation, roof.
Important: Maintenance does not imply repairs that are covered by [[Home Insurance and Emergencies]] policies. But may include additional post remedial work required. These are harder to predict.

Planned expenditure could be kept in a separate account, ring-fenced solely for this and separate to emergency funds. For example £30k-50k in a high-interest account with limited drawdown. This can be flexible depending on when it needs to be available as liquidity versus opportunity for growth.
