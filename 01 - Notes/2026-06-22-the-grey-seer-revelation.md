# The Grey Seer Revelation — A Contrast Painting Diary

## Premise

This isn't a tutorial. It's the record of working something out.

I needed a way to paint Ultramarines infantry at Legions Imperialis scale — dozens of 6mm models — without the process becoming a grind. What I landed on was a single coat of Talassar Blue contrast over Grey Seer primer. It works better than it has any right to. This is why.

---

## Core Argument

The insight wasn't Talassar Blue. Everyone knows Talassar Blue is a good Ultramarine colour. The insight was Grey Seer *first* — that the primer is doing active work before paint touches the model, giving you a cold blue-grey undertone that Talassar Blue then enriches rather than fighting against.

Most contrast painting tutorials talk about what contrast does. This is about what the primer does. The primer is half the painting.

Beyond the technique: there's something worth writing about in the experience of batch-painting a force at this scale. The rhythm of it. The moment where "good enough" stops being a compromise and starts being a genuine standard.
