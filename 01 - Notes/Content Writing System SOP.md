# Content Writing System SOP

**Goal:** Turn your notes and ideas into published content — from raw idea to finished piece, then repurposed into 10–30 short-form posts.

### Workflow Summary
1. Select an idea
2. Use the right template
3. Brain dump
4. Research your vault
5. Ideation
6. Draft outline
7. Hook brainstorm
8. Write first draft
9. Review and refine
10. Repurpose

---

### Step 1: Select an Idea

Browse your permanent notes, literature notes, or `00 - Inbox/`. Pick the idea you have the most energy for today.

Energy > logic when choosing what to write. The best piece is the one you're genuinely excited to make.

---

### Step 2: Use the Right Template

- **Newsletter** → `Newsletter Template`
- **YouTube video** → `YouTube Template`
- **Tweet batch** → `Weekly Tweets Template`

Press `Cmd+N` → `Cmd+P` → "Insert template" → pick the right one. The correct `categories:` YAML is already pre-filled.

---

### Step 3: Brain Dump

**Option A — Manual:** Write everything you know about the topic for 20 minutes. No filter. No structure. Just ideas on the page.

**Option B — AI voice note (faster):**
1. Record a 3–10 min voice note — speak freely, as if explaining the idea to a friend
2. Get the transcript (most phone apps auto-transcribe)
3. Feed to Claude: *"Structure this as a [newsletter / tweet / YouTube outline]. Here are 2–3 writing samples of mine: [paste examples]."*
4. Review — AI handles the structure, you bring the soul

---

### Step 4: Research Your Vault

Before going outside, search inside first:

- Use `Cmd+O` (Quick Switcher) to find relevant permanent notes
- Open related literature notes in a side pane
- Copy useful highlights or ideas into the Research section of your template

This is the payoff of building your second brain — your past thinking becomes raw material for your current piece.

---

### Step 5: Ideation

Fill in whichever elements fit (not all are required):

- **Problem** — What pain or struggle does this address?
- **Goal** — What does the reader want to achieve?
- **Example** — A personal story or experience that makes it relatable
- **Process** — Actionable steps
- **Big Idea** — The one sentence that captures the whole thing
- **Concept** — A memorable name for your idea or framework

---

### Step 6: Draft Outline

Open the [[Copywriting Structure]] note. Pick the framework that fits:
- **PAS** — Problem, Aggravate, Solution
- **APAG** — Attention, Perspective, Advantage, Gamify
- **What-Why-How** — Simple and clear

Copy the structure into your document. Fill in your ideation elements.

---

### Step 7: Hook Brainstorm

Write 5–10 possible hooks, subject lines, or opening sentences. Pick the best one. The hook determines 80% of your results.

---

### Step 8: Write the First Draft

Open the outline in a side pane. Write the full draft in the main pane. Don't edit while writing — just flow.

---

### Step 9: Review and Refine

- Read out loud — rewrite anything you stumble on
- Cut 10–20% for conciseness
- Ask Claude to review specific sections if stuck
- Finalize

---

### Step 10: Repurpose

From every finished piece, extract 5–10 standalone ideas. Each becomes a tweet or Substack note. Use the **AI Content Acceleration System** prompt to do this in bulk.

---

**Recommended weekly schedule:**
- Monday: Outline + vault research
- Tuesday: First draft
- Wednesday: Review + refine
- Thursday: Repurpose into tweets/posts
- Friday: Plan next week's content
